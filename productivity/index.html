All my productivity tools increase the speed at which I can perform a given task. That's essentially the point of programming. Sure, it can be done, but why would you spend 5s to do it, when it can be done in 1s if you learned something new?

The most wasteful part of wasted time is the mental down time between productivity and distracted. While you may spend 5s being distracted, the mental down time can actually be 30s, which is huge.

However, my personal goal is to decrease the number of clicks and keyboard presses and keyboard-to-mouse transitions. While Vim and Emacs do that, they sacrifice a lot of other functionality. Maybe I'll rewire my brain into them for later, but for now.

We all have a finite number of keyboard presses in our lifetime, so we should do our best to increase the output of each press.

We should try to do as much as we can with as little effort as we can manage, so we don't waste our valuable keypresses doing something that could've been done easier.
