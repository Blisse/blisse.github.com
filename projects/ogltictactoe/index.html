This is a 99% complete tic-tac-toe game I made in OpenGL with OpenGLUT. I say it's 99% because I haven't implemented keyboard actions beyond quitting via ESC, and I don't plan to. This is enough for a small taste of OpenGL, and while there was a lot to learn, it just never felt right to program in.

OpenGL relies heavily on matrices to draw onto the screen, something that I'm not familiar with at all. My implementation of it is extremely simple, and most of all, 2D.

Motivation

The basic game for a programmer is a console tic-tac-toe games, which really isn't that hard. So why don't I step it up a notch? I learn OpenGL, I include 3 levels of AI difficulty and the ability to select X or O. I'm pretty happy with the end result, knowledge and program-wise.

You can find my project here.

D3D v. OpenGL

OpenGL was always really interesting to me, partially because of my long time playing Counter-Strike. Because I am a tinkerer, I always venture into the game settings to take a look at what options are available.. A very interesting observation I remember, is that there were at least two options in the video settings. D3D and OpenGL.

As a kid, I never really knew what they were, but one day when I had some free time to think about what to program, all these thoughts of Counter-Strike rushed back at me, and I decided to take a farther look at OpenGL solutions. This is the result, and I will save DirectX for another time.

I now realize that there was and still is a huge debate between using the two API's, and while I'm not entirely sure exactly what's going on, I still find it very interesting, and maybe you will too. DirectX is winning by a huge margin though at the moment because of how hard Microsoft has pushed it forward, though some believe if we had only gone OpenGL to begin with, our rendering technology would be leaps and bounds where it is today.
